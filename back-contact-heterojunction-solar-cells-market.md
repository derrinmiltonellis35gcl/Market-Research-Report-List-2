<p><p>Back-contact heterojunction solar cells are a type of solar cell design where all electrical contacts are on the rear side of the cell, allowing for higher efficiency and reduced shading. There are three main types of back-contact heterojunction solar cells in the market: IBC (Interdigitated Back Contact) solar cells, EWT (Emitter Wrap Through) solar cells, and MWT (Metal Wrap Through) solar cells. Each type has its own unique design and manufacturing process, leading to differences in efficiency, cost, and application suitability.</p></p>
