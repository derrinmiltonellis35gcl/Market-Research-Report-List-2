<p><p>Micro Nuclear Reactors (MNRs) are small-scale nuclear reactors designed to provide power to isolated or off-grid areas. There are three main types of MNRs: High Temperature Reactors, which use helium or molten salt as coolant; Molten Salt Reactors, which use molten fluoride salts as fuel and coolant; and Liquid Metal Reactors (LMRs), which use liquid metal such as sodium or lead as coolant. Each type has its own unique advantages and applications, catering to different market needs and preferences.</p></p>
