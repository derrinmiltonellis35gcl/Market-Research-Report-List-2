<p><p>Inductive sensors are devices that use the principle of electromagnetic induction to detect the presence or absence of an object. There are two main types of inductive sensors: DC operated and AC operated. DC operated LVDT sensors typically require a direct current power source and are commonly used in applications where precision and accuracy are key. On the other hand, AC operated LVDT sensors utilize an alternating current power source and are often preferred for their reliability and stability in harsh industrial environments.</p></p>
